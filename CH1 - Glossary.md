**Algebraic structure** – A mathematical object (like a group, ring, or field) defined by a set and operations that satisfy certain rules.

**Group theory** – The branch of mathematics that studies groups, which are sets with an operation that combines elements according to specific axioms.

**Finite group** – A group with a finite number of elements.

**Discrete group** – A group viewed as a topological space where each element is isolated (no “continuous” structure).

**Lattice** – In group theory or geometry, a regular repeating arrangement of points in space, often related to symmetries.

**Topological group** – A group that also has a topological structure, where the group operations (multiplication and inverse) are continuous.

**Lie group** – A group that is also a smooth manifold, allowing the use of calculus; crucial for studying continuous symmetries.

**Solenoid (group)** – A type of topological group that is compact, connected, and one-dimensional, often used as an example in topology.

**Circle group (U(1))** – The group of complex numbers with absolute value 1, under multiplication; represents rotations in 2D.

**General linear group (GL(n))** – The group of all invertible n×n matrices with entries from a field.

**Special linear group (SL(n))** – The subgroup of GL(n) with determinant exactly 1.

**Orthogonal group (O(n))** – The group of n×n real matrices that preserve lengths (rotations and reflections).

**Euclidean group (E(n))** – The group of rigid motions (translations + rotations/reflections) in n-dimensional Euclidean space.

**Special orthogonal group (SO(n))** – The subgroup of O(n) that preserves orientation (pure rotations).

**Unitary group (U(n))** – The group of n×n complex matrices that preserve the standard inner product.

**Special unitary group (SU(n))** – The subgroup of U(n) with determinant 1; central in quantum physics.

**Symplectic group (Sp(n))** – The group of matrices that preserve a symplectic form, important in classical mechanics and geometry.

**Exceptional Lie groups (G₂, F₄, E₆, E₇, E₈)** – Rare, highly symmetric Lie groups beyond the classical families.

**Lorentz group** – The group of transformations preserving spacetime intervals in relativity.

**Poincaré group** – The group of all spacetime symmetries in special relativity (Lorentz + translations).

**Conformal group** – Transformations that preserve angles but not necessarily distances.

**Diffeomorphism group** – The group of smooth, invertible maps of a manifold onto itself.

**Loop group** – A group of maps from the circle into a Lie group, used in string theory.

**Infinite-dimensional Lie group** – A Lie group whose underlying manifold is infinite-dimensional.

**Algebraic group** – A group defined by polynomial equations, studied in algebraic geometry.

**Classical groups** – The four main families of matrix Lie groups: GL, SL, O/SO, U/SU, Sp.

**Simple Lie group** – A connected Lie group with no nontrivial normal subgroups, except the group itself and the identity.

**Lie algebra** – The tangent space at the identity of a Lie group, with a bracket operation encoding the group’s structure.

**Semisimple Lie algebra** – A Lie algebra that can be decomposed into simple Lie algebras.

**Representation theory** – The study of how groups (or algebras) act as transformations on vector spaces.

**Standard inner product** – The dot product generalized to complex vector spaces.

**Normal subgroup** – A subgroup invariant under conjugation, allowing the formation of quotient groups.

**Compact group** – A group that is closed and bounded as a topological space; ensures finiteness properties.

**Simply connected** – A space with no “holes,” meaning every loop can be continuously shrunk to a point.

**Cyclic group (ℤ/nℤ)** – A group generated by a single element with n possible powers.

**Root of unity** – A complex number that, when raised to some integer power n, equals 1.

**Outer automorphism** – A symmetry of a group that cannot be achieved by conjugating inside the group.

**Maximal torus** – A largest abelian subgroup of a Lie group, consisting of diagonal matrices of determinant 1 in SU(n).

**Weyl group** – The finite group generated by symmetries of the maximal torus, often permutations.

**Symmetric group (Sₙ)** – The group of all permutations of n elements.

**Hermitian matrix** – A complex square matrix equal to its conjugate transpose.

**Anti-Hermitian (skew-Hermitian) matrix** – A complex matrix equal to the negative of its conjugate transpose.

**Traceless matrix** – A matrix whose diagonal elements add to zero.

**Commutator \[A,B]** – The expression AB − BA, measuring how two matrices fail to commute.

**Anticommutator {A,B}** – The expression AB + BA, often used in quantum mechanics.

**Structure constants (fᵃᵦ꜀, dᵃᵦ꜀)** – Numbers that define the multiplication rules of basis elements in a Lie algebra.

**Jacobi identity** – A fundamental condition that Lie brackets must satisfy, ensuring consistency.

**Generator** – A basic matrix or operator from which the entire group can be built by exponentiation.

**Pauli matrices (σ₁, σ₂, σ₃)** – Three special 2×2 Hermitian matrices used in quantum mechanics to describe spin.

**Gell-Mann matrices (λ₁–λ₈)** – Eight traceless Hermitian 3×3 matrices used as generators of SU(3).

**Adjoint representation** – A way a group acts on its own Lie algebra via commutators.

**Quaternion** – A 4D number system extending complex numbers, useful for rotations in 3D.

**Versor** – A unit quaternion (norm 1), representing a rotation.

**Surjective homomorphism** – A structure-preserving map between groups that covers the entire target group.

**Kernel of a homomorphism** – The set of elements mapped to the identity; measures redundancy.

**Spin group (Spin(n))** – A double cover of SO(n), used to describe spin in quantum theory.

**Fiber bundle** – A topological construction where a “fiber space” is attached smoothly to each point of a “base space.”

**Homotopy group (πₙ)** – A mathematical object measuring the kinds of “holes” in a space.

**Cartan subalgebra** – A maximal abelian subalgebra of a Lie algebra, often consisting of diagonal matrices.

**Roots (of a Lie algebra)** – Vectors describing how basis elements transform under the Cartan subalgebra.

**Dynkin diagram** – A graph encoding the structure of simple Lie algebras.

**Cartan matrix** – A matrix that encodes the relations among simple roots.

**Bloch sphere** – A geometric representation of the quantum states of a single qubit.
